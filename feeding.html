The Diet

A bearded dragon’s diet is incredibly important to their health and growth, yet often at big chain pet stores their diet is incredibly undermined. Commonly, it gets boiled down to super worms as a primary feeder insect and the vague fact that they can eat fruits and veggies. While bearded dragons are omnivores, meaning they consume both plants and animals, they are primarily insectivores. This means that while they can eat a wide variety of foods like fruits and veggies, their staple foods should consist of nutritious and protein packed insects. A bearded dragon’s age also plays a considerable role in their diet and what they can and can’t eat, such as an adult bearded dragon’s diet shifting to a more plant and vegetable focused one.


The Foods

Because of how wide the assortment of foods bearded dragons can eat is, it might seem hard to pick the right foods. So, what can and can’t they eat exactly? Vegetables and greens are incredibly healthy to a bearded dragon, such as kale and cabbage, and they also loved collared greens. Some also enjoy carrots, however they can cause runny stools. In terms of fruits, they can enjoy apples, grapes, apricots, and blue berries, although blue berries should be used as treats. Generally, any fruit or vegetable with high sugar should be fed sparingly, and anything citrus such as oranges should be completely avoided. Anything with high phosphorus should also be avoided. Insects should be a dragon’s primary source of food, and ideally you can use crickets because of their protein and calcium or dubia roaches. Super worms, commonly advocated at pet stores, are good when used in moderation or treats, but not as a main source because of their high fat content which can lead to obesity and isn’t good for babies. It’s important that when you aren’t sure what to feed your beardy that you do a quick look up, as not all foods are equal, and your bearded dragons age will impact what you feed them and how often.
